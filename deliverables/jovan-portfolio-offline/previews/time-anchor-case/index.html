A diary people wanted to keep—without the friction that made them stop.

01 · OBSERVE

The real problem was continuity.

At an offline journaling event, I heard the same tension several times: physical five-year diaries felt meaningful, but they were difficult to carry, offered little feedback, and made progress hard to see.

I reframed the challenge from “make journaling more emotional” to reducing friction while preserving the seriousness of writing something down.
02 · PRODUCT RESPONSE

Three decisions shaped the experience.

  1. 01
    Make time visible.

    A same-day five-year timeline supports reflection; an annual memory map makes accumulated effort visible.

  2. 02
    Make memory searchable.

    Keyword search helps users rediscover themes and changes that are difficult to notice one entry at a time.

  3. 03
    Protect the original moment.

    Primary entries are locked. Later understanding appears as a follow-up note, preserving both versions of the self.
